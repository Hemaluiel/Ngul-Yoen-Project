I am Hema Luitel, intern at Bhutan Data Scientists Pvt. Ltd.

Ngul Yoen is the project aimed at providing insights into the banking sector in Bhutan.

I have examined customer banking choices in Bhutan using survey data from 111 respondents. It finds that trust, service quality, accessibility, and ease of use are the main factors influencing decisions, with the Bank of Bhutan being the most preferred. While most customers are satisfied, some face issues like limited access, network problems, and lack of awareness. Many users are also uncertain about staying with their current bank, indicating room for improvement. The study suggests enhancing digital services, accessibility, and financial literacy to improve customer satisfaction.

The findings shows that most people prefer the Bank of Bhutan, indicating a strong market dominance. Overall, customers are generally satisfied with their banks, especially in terms of trust and service quality. However, a small group still faces issues such as security concerns, poor service experiences, and lack of clear communication.
