Customer Banking Choices

Kuzuzangpo la! Explore key insights on the customer banking choices in Bhutan.

Key Findings

  1. Most respondents (103 out of 111) have bank accounts, but a small group remains unbanked due to lack of awareness, income, or age limitations.
  2. The Bank of Bhutan (BOB) is the most preferred bank, with about 84% of respondents choosing it.
  3. Key factors influencing bank choice include trust, service quality, accessibility, and ease of use.Trust is a major factor, with over 80% of customers expressing confidence in their bank, though a few still have security concerns.
  4. Service quality is generally good, but around 7–10% of users reported dissatisfaction, indicating gaps that need improvement.
  5. Accessibility varies by location: rural users face limited access to ATMs/branches, while urban users experience congestion.
  6. Ease of use of digital banking is positive overall, but network issues and lack of awareness affect some users.
  7. A significant number of customers are uncertain or willing to switch banks, showing opportunities for banks to improve services and retain customers.
  8. There is a clear gap in financial literacy and awareness about available banking services.
