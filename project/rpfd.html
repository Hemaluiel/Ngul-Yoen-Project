Introduction

Context and background of the study

Banking services are central to Bhutan's growing digital economy. With the rapid adoption of mobile and internet banking, customer expectations are evolving — and the gap between what banks offer and what customers actually need has become measurable.

Problem statement

Customers experience meaningful differences across Bhutan's banks in four dimensions: trust, service quality, accessibility, and ease of use. This study quantifies those differences and maps what drives customer choice.

Research questions

What factors most influence a customer's choice of bank?
Where do current banking services fall short of expectations?
What improvements would most increase customer satisfaction and loyalty?
🔬

Conclusion

Summary of key takeaways

Trust, service quality, accessibility, and ease of use are the four pillars that most strongly shape customer banking choices in Bhutan. Of these, ease of use — particularly around digital and mobile banking — presents the greatest opportunity for improvement across the sector.

With a significant share of customers undecided about switching banks, the competitive window for improvement is open. Banks that invest in transparent communication, reliable digital infrastructure, and expanded rural reach stand to gain the most in customer loyalty and new account acquisition in the years ahead.

Ngul Yoen exists to bridge the information gap — helping customers make informed choices, and giving banks a clearer picture of what their customers actually value.

Key findings

8 headline insights from 111 respondents · Bhutan 2026

Kuzuzangpo la! The survey captured responses from customers across Bhutan's major banks. Here are the most important patterns that emerged from the data.

1
103 of 111 respondents have bank accounts — but a small group remains unbanked due to lack of awareness, insufficient income, or age limitations.
2
Bank of Bhutan (BoB) dominates customer choice with approximately 84% of respondents selecting it as their primary bank.
3
The four key factors influencing bank choice are trust, service quality, accessibility, and ease of use — in that order of stated importance.
4
Over 80% of customers trust their bank — but security concerns around digital transactions remain a notable anxiety for many users.
5
Service quality is generally positive, but 7–10% of users reported dissatisfaction — mainly around long wait times and insufficient staff support for digital queries.
6
Accessibility varies sharply by location: rural users face limited ATM and branch access, while urban users experience branch congestion and long queues.
7
Digital banking ease of use is rated positively overall, but network reliability issues reduce the experience significantly in rural and semi-urban areas.
8
Many customers are uncertain or open to switching banks — a clear competitive opportunity for institutions that invest in customer experience improvements.
