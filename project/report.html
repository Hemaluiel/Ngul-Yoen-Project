Customer Banking Choices in Bhutan

Understanding Trust, Service Quality, Accessibility & Ease of Use

Introduction

Banking services are essential in Bhutan’s growing digital economy. With increasing use of mobile and internet banking, customer expectations are rapidly evolving.

Problem Statement

Customers experience differences in trust, service quality, accessibility, and ease of use across banks.

Main Motives

  • Understand banking choices
  • Identify service gaps
  • Provide solutions

Research Questions

  • What influences banking choices?
  • What needs improvement?

Methodology

Data collected from 111 respondents using surveys. Tools: Google Forms, Sheets, Tableau, Website.

Findings & Analysis

Overview

Most people have bank accounts, but some lack awareness.

Tableau Chart Here

Customer Banking Choices

Bank of Bhutan dominates with ~84% preference.

Bank Preference Chart

Trust

Over 80% trust their bank, but security concerns exist.

Service Quality

Majority satisfied, but small dissatisfaction exists.

Accessibility

Urban congestion & rural access issues observed.

Ease of Use

Digital services good, but network issues affect usage.

Future Insight

Many customers are unsure about switching banks, showing opportunity for improvement.

Discussion & Solutions

Banking Assistance Website

  • Compare banks
  • Learn services
  • Make better decisions

Suggestions to BOB

  • Improve internet banking
  • Add budgeting tools

Conclusion

Trust, service quality, accessibility, and ease of use strongly influence customer banking choices. Improving these can increase satisfaction and loyalty.
